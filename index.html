OUR HISTORY

Since 1969, education has been the core aspect of conservation efforts. To inspire young minds for conservation action and connect them with nature, the Nature Clubs of India was started in 1976 which successfully continued to motivate thousands of individuals over three decades.

Recognising the role of children and youth as major stakeholders, WWF-India re-strategised its education work and created programmes to build a generation of environmentally conscious individuals. The strategy follows a three-pronged approach of building environmental awareness and knowledge; developing requisite capacity and skills; and enabling action for conservation and sustainability.

The five education programmes are currently running in 16 states across India, taking within its purview all members of society across all age groups.

Environmental history is a multidisciplinary field that draws on the humanities and natural sciences. It emerged in the United States in the 1960s and 1970s as a response to growing awareness of environmental issues, such as air and water pollution, the ozone layer depletion, and the greenhouse effect.
Environmental history is the study of the relationship between humans and the natural world over time. It examines how humans have shaped nature, how nature has influenced humans, and how the two have interacted.

SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT

Sustainable development meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

Key Principles:

  • 1. Interconnectedness: Recognizing the interdependence of economic, social, and environmental systems.
  • 2. Equity: Ensuring fairness and equality for all people, promoting social inclusion.
  • 3. Environmental Protection: Conserving natural resources and protecting ecosystems for future generations.
  • 4. Sustainable Economic Growth: Supporting economic activities that are environmentally sound and socially inclusive.

Importance of Sustainable Development:

It is crucial for ensuring long-term ecological balance and human well-being, addressing climate change, and promoting resilience in communities.

Examples of Sustainable Development:

  • 1. Renewable energy projects (solar, wind, hydropower).
  • 2. Sustainable agriculture practices (organic farming, permaculture).
  • 3. Eco-friendly urban planning (green buildings, public transport).
